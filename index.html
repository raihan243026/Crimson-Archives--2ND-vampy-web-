Count Dracula

The most infamous of all vampires, Count Dracula emerged from the mists of Transylvania to become the archetypal lord of the undead. Born from Bram Stoker's imagination yet rooted in ancient folklore, Dracula represents the eternal struggle between civilization and primal darkness. His castle stands as a monument to centuries of supernatural power, where time itself bends to his immortal will.

Nosferatu

Count Orlok, known as Nosferatu, embodies the more grotesque and terrifying aspects of vampirism. Unlike his more charismatic counterparts, Nosferatu represents pure horror - a plague-bringing creature of the night whose very presence brings death and decay. His elongated fingers and rat-like features serve as a reminder that not all monsters hide behind beautiful facades.
